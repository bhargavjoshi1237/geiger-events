# Geiger Events — Product Overview

*A modern event platform for communities and small organisers: beautiful pages, fast ticketing, social momentum, and reliable check-in — without enterprise pricing.*

This is a plain-language tour of what Geiger Events does. No code, no jargon — just what you can actually do with it, whether you're the person running events or the person buying tickets.

---

## What Geiger Events is

Geiger Events is an all-in-one tool for creating events, selling tickets, managing your guests, and running the door on the day. It's built for creators, communities, local businesses, workshops, nightlife, meetups, and small conferences — the kind of organisers who want serious ticketing power without paying for (or fighting with) an enterprise platform.

It's part of the wider **Geiger suite**, so it shares the same clean, consistent look and feel as the other Geiger apps.

There are two sides to the product:

1. **The organiser workspace** — where you build and run your events.
2. **The attendee experience** — the public event pages your buyers see, and the members portal where they manage everything they've bought.

---

## For organisers — the workspace

Everything below is a real, working part of the product. You sign in, pick your project, and work from a sidebar organised by area.

### Overview
Your home dashboard. See the numbers that matter at a glance — registrations, revenue, check-ins, trends — and a roster of your events.

### Events
The heart of the product.
- **All Events** — create and manage every event, each with its own editor for cover media, tickets, visibility, location and sharing.
- **Templates** — save an event setup and reuse it so you're not starting from scratch each time.
- **Event Series** — group recurring or related events together.
- **Event Wall** — a public, branded page listing all your events that fans can browse.

### Venues
Build a library of reusable venues with their own capacity details, then pick one when you create an event. The venue automatically fills in the location and shows on the public page.

### Registrations
Everything about *getting people signed up*:
- **RSVPs** and **Registration Forms** — collect the exact details you need.
- **Waitlist** — automatically hold people when you're full and promote them when space opens.
- **Approval Gates** — review and approve who gets in.
- **Capacity Limits** — cap numbers so you never oversell.
- **Dietary & Accessibility** — collect requirements up front.
- Plus finer controls like plus-ones, member-only or token-gated sign-up, conditional questions, deadlines, and custom confirmation pages.

### Guests (your CRM)
A single contact database that grows with every event.
- **Contact Book** — the hub. Import contacts, find duplicates, block unwanted emails.
- **Guest List** — your all-time roster. **Who's Going** — the upcoming-only view.
- **Segments** and **Tags** — group your audience however you like.
- **Notes** — keep running notes on contacts.
- **Attendee Export** — pull the data out any way you need.
- **Data Requests** — handle privacy/consent requests cleanly.

### Tickets
The deepest area in the product — reusable ticketing building blocks you set up once and attach to any event:
- Ticket types, tiers, and bundles
- Discounts & promo codes, early-bird sales, dynamic pricing
- Donations, group purchasing, access-code tickets, reserved seating
- Anti-scalping & resale controls, multi-currency
- Payment methods, payment plans, payouts, taxes, refunds, and ticket transfers
- A cross-event **Orders & Attendees** roster, plus invoices & receipts

### Memberships
Offer recurring plans that unlock special pricing or access. Define the plans and their perks, see who's enrolled, and control how members join.

### Check-in
Everything for running the door on the day:
- QR tickets and Apple/Google-style wallet passes
- A check-in app, door sales, offline check-in, and kiosk mode
- Badge printing, session check-in, RFID/NFC, self check-in
- Multi-gate & zones, real-time attendance, staff scanning roles, lead retrieval, and name-search lookup

### Campaigns
Reach your audience across channels:
- Newsletters, email/SMS/WhatsApp invites, and text blasts
- Automated reminders and drip sequences
- An email template builder, segmentation, send scheduling, A/B testing
- Deliverability and personalisation controls

### Advertising
A simple, layman-friendly control panel over the big ad platforms (Google Ads, Meta Ads, Facebook Marketplace, Google AdSense). Connect your accounts, run ad campaigns, set budgets, and see performance in one place.

### Workflows
A no-code automation engine. Build "when this happens, do that" flows with a visual step list and drag-and-drop canvas, start from ready-made templates, and review a history of every run.

### Conference
Tools for running a proper conference or summit — agenda builder, speakers, sponsors and sponsorship packages, expo booths, call for papers, recordings, plus venue/housing sourcing and CEU certificates.

### Community
Keep your audience engaged with polls, surveys, and announcements.

### Discovery
Your public organiser profile, shown on your Event Wall, that fans can follow to hear about new events.

### Messages
An inbox where you read and reply to support conversations your buyers start from their portal — questions about tickets, orders, refunds and more.

### Analytics
Set up scheduled reports delivered on a recurring basis. (Deeper dashboards — sales, attendance, funnels, forecasting — are on the roadmap.)

### Settings
Manage your team, roles & permissions, API keys & webhooks, and custom domains.

## For attendees — the public pages and members portal

### The public event page
This is what you share with the world. On it, a buyer can:
- See the full event — cover image, date, time, venue, and whether it's in-person, online, or hybrid.
- Browse ticket types and prices, grouped by tier.
- Pick quantities, choose time slots, and add extras like merchandise, meal choices, or add-ons.
- Apply discount codes (early-bird pricing applies automatically), add a donation, or get member pricing if they're a member.
- Answer any custom or dietary/accessibility questions you've set.
- Check out — free tickets confirm instantly; paid tickets go through secure Stripe payment.
- Land on a confirmation with an order code, or a "pending approval" / "waitlisted" status if that's how you've set the event up.
- Share the event with a tap.

### Group buying
Buyers can purchase for a whole group in one go, entering each attendee's name and email, with any group discount applied automatically.

### The members portal
Every buyer gets a personal portal to manage everything they've bought:
- **Home** — a hero card for their next event with a countdown, plus quick stats and recent orders.
- **Tickets** — upcoming and past tickets, each opening to a scannable **QR code**, full purchase breakdown, and buttons to add to calendar, get directions, or message the organiser.
- **Orders** — searchable, filterable order history with itemised receipts.
- **Memberships** — view active plans and renewal dates, and browse and join new ones.
- **Messages** — a threaded, two-way conversation with the organiser.
- **Notifications** — announcements from organisers they follow or have bought from.
- **Account** — update their profile, change their password, and sign out of devices.

### Refunds
Buyers can request a refund on an eligible order, add a reason, and track the status (requested → approved/denied → refunded) right from the portal — and the organiser handles it from their side.

### The organiser wall & following
Your public **Event Wall** (`/w/your-name`) shows your branding, bio, links, and a filterable, sortable grid of your events with featured highlights. Visitors can **follow** you with just an email to get notified whenever you publish something new.

### Signing in
A simple, password-based sign-in built around email. New members set a password from an emailed link, and there's a straightforward forgot-password flow.

## Who it's for

- **Creators and communities** running meetups, workshops, and recurring events.
- **Local businesses and venues** selling tickets to nightlife, classes, or shows.
- **Small conferences** that need agendas, speakers, sponsors, and check-in.
- Anyone who has outgrown a basic RSVP tool but doesn't want the cost and complexity of an enterprise suite.

---

## Where it sits

Geiger Events deliberately lives between two worlds:

- **Lighter, cheaper, and more social** than enterprise platforms like Cvent, Bizzabo, and Whova.
- **More polished and organiser-friendly** than generic RSVP or basic ticketing tools like Evite or a bare Eventbrite listing.

You get the beautiful pages and social momentum of a modern community app, the ticketing depth and day-of operations of a serious platform, and transparent pricing that makes sense for someone running their own events.
